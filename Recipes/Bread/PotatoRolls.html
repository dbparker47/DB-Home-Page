Grandma's Potato Rolls (Joy)
    1 1/2 cups warm water (110-115 degrees)
    1 package Active dry yeast (1/4 oz)
    2/3 cup sugar
    1 teaspoon salt
    2/3 cup soft shortening
    2 eggs
    1 cup lukewarm mashed potatoes
    7 to 7 1/2 cups flour
  • In bowl, dissolve yeast in water. (you can add about 1/2 teaspoon sugar to get it to grow better.) Stir in sugar, salt, shortening, eggs, and potatoes. Mix in flour with hand until dough is easy to handle. (you can use a dough hook on the Kitchen Aid.)
  • Turn onto lightly floured board. Knead until smooth and elastic. (you can do all but the last bit of kneading with the dough hook.) Place greased side up in a greased bowl. Cover with damp cloth. Place in refrigerator.
  • About 2 hours before baking, shape dough into rolls. (you can make 3 small balls to make clover-leafed rolls.) Cover and let rise until double, 1 1/2 to 2 hours. Bake at 400 degrees for 11 minutes (13 minutes for pyrex cake pan). Makes 4 dozen medium rolls.
