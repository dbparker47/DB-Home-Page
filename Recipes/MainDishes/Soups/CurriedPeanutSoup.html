Curried Peanut Soup
    Soup:
    1 T butter
    2 cloves of crushed garlic
    1 cup finely chopped onion
    2 teaspoons freshly grated ginger root
    1 teaspoon salt
    1 cup chopped raw peanuts
    1/4 teaspoon each: cinnamon, cloves, ground cardamom or coriander
    1/2 teaspoon each: dry mustard, turmeric
    1 teaspoon ground cumin
    2 cups stock
    1/2 cups natural peanut butter
    1 tablespoon honey
    1/4-1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
    1 1/2 cup room temperature buttermilk
    Topping:
    2 fairly green bananas
    juice of 1 lemon
    3 tablespoons butter
    1/4 teaspoon each: cinnamon, turmeric
    dash of salt
  • In a large saucepan saute the first 5 items together over medium heat. Add the peanuts and spices and saute, stirring another 10 minutes over medium low heat.
  • Add the stock, peanut butter, honey, and cayenne to the saute. Mix and cover. Simmer over sheepishly low heat for 1 hour. Stir occasionally.
  • Whisk the buttermilk into the hot soup just a few minutes before serving.
  • Peel the bananas and slice them thinly on the diagonal. Place them in a shallow bowl to bathe in fresh lemon juice. Let it stand 10 minutes.
  • Heat the butter in a heavy skillet. Add the bananas plus all their lemon juice, and sprinkle with salt and spices.
  • Fry, gently turning and stirring, 5-8 minutes over medium-low heat.
  • Spoon the topping into each individual bowl of soup. You will not regret this seemingly strange taste experience.
