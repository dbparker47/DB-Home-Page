Black Bean Soup
    1 1/2 cups dried black beans (15 oz. can)
    3 celery ribs, chopped
    3 medium carrots, chopped
    1 large onion, chopped
    2 garlic cloves, minced
    2 t olive oil
    6 1/2 cups broth
    1 t dried oregano
    1/2 t dried thyme
    1/2 t salt
    1/4 t cayenne pepper
    1 bay leaf
    3 T lime juice
    8 T sour cream or plain yogurt
    1/4 cup minced fresh cilantro (garnish)
  • Place beans in a large saucepan; add water to cover by 2 in. Bring to a boil; boil for 2 minutes. Remove from heat; cover and let stand for 1 to 4 hours or until beans are softened. Drain and rinse beans, discarding liquid.
  • In a Dutch oven, saute the celery, carrots, onion, and garlic in oil patiently. Add the beans, broth, oregano, thyme, salt, cayenne, and bay leaf. Bring to a boil. Reduce heat; cover and simmer for 1 to 1 1/4 hours or until beans are tender. Discard bay leaf. Cool slightly.
  • In a blender, cover and process soup in batches until smooth. Return to pan; heat through. Stir in lime juice. Garnish each serving with sour cream or yogurt and cilantro.
